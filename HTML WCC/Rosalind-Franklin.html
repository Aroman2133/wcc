Rosalind Franklin



"Science, for me, gives a partial explanation of life ... I do not accept your definition of faith i.e. belief in life after death ... Your faith rests on the future of yourself and others as individuals, mine in the future and fate of our successors. It seems to me that yours is the more selfish." -Rosalind Franklin"

Born in 1920's London, Rosalind Franklin showed exceptional intelligence from a young age, being called "alarmingly clever" by her family. After entrance into Newnham College, Cambridge, Franklin studeied chemistry and was awarded second class honors for her final exams. Post graduation, Franklin continued her passions at the physical chemistry laboratory of the University of Cambridge. During her work there, she helped "classify coals and accurately predict their performance for fuel purposes and for production of wartime devices such as gas masks" during WWII.

Franklin Facts

  1. In 1950, Franklin was granted a three-year Turner and Newall Fellowship to work at King's College London.
  2. Franklin and her colleague Raymond Gosling discovered that there were two forms of DNA.
  3. Franklin is best known for her work on the X-ray diffraction images of DNA, particularly Photo 51.
